Autonomous Robot

2018-2020

I was heavily involved with RPI's premier robotics team, the Rock Raiders. I primairly worked on the robot software, which included:

  1. A companion simulator. I wrote it to present the same APIs as our ROS device drivers, so it was a drop-in replacement for the robot hardware. It was incredibly usefulf when debugging our localization and navigation stack
  2. Localization. I worked on our sensor fusion strategy, so deciding which sensors are fused into which coordinate frames, and how they were fed into our path planner and obstacle map. GPS was a lot less useful than I thought it would be
  3. Vision. IGVC required driving within painted white lanes on a grassy field. I worked extensively on our lane detector
  4. Hardware integration. This was mostly writing ROS nodes to communicate with sensors and motor drivers
  5. Fixing random problems. For example, I found a bug in the firmware of our motor drivers where they would only output half power (it was very weird)
I also did electrical and mechanical work here-and-there.
